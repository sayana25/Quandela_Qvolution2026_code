Figure 1 — QRC Methodology Pipeline. The pipeline has four stages. (1) Data: the Iris dataset is standardised and angle-encoded; the Swaptions volatility surface is compressed from 224 dimensions to 6 PCA latent scores and arranged as next-day lag pairs. (2) Quantum Reservoir: inputs are encoded into a fixed random quantum circuit — a qubit circuit in PennyLane or a photonic circuit in MerLin — whose weights are never updated. The circuit generates a high-dimensional feature vector through quantum interference and entanglement (PauliZ expectation values for PennyLane; Fock state probabilities for MerLin). (3) Classical Readout: a linear layer or small MLP is trained on the quantum features using standard gradient descent; the quantum circuit is not involved in this stage. (4) Evaluation: QRC variants are compared against classical baselines and against each other through a systematic ablation isolating the effect of photon number, mode count, entanglement depth, and reservoir ensembling.
